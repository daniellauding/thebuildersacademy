# Phase 1: Business Goals & Strategic Foundation

**Project:** The Builders Academy Redesign
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Status:** Phase 1 Complete

---

## 1. Core Purpose

The Builders Academy exists to **provide accessible, accredited online construction and safety training** that enables builders, tradespeople, and employers across the UK to meet regulatory requirements, gain industry certifications, and advance their careers — quickly, affordably, and entirely online.

**Core value proposition:** Same-day certificates, CPD-accredited courses, and CITB exam access — completed online at your own pace, from any device.

---

## 2. Conversion Goals (Prioritized)

### Primary Goals

1. **Increase individual course purchases** — Most visitors land directly on product pages via Google. The #1 job is converting that intent into a sale with minimal friction.
2. **Increase employer/bulk package sales** — Higher AOV opportunity. Construction companies need to train multiple staff members. Currently no visible bulk purchase pathway exists.
3. **Increase trust and credibility** — The target audience (builders, 20-60+, mixed digital literacy) needs strong trust signals before entering payment details on an unfamiliar site.

### Secondary Goals

4. **Improve course discovery** — Users who arrive at the homepage or a category page should quickly find relevant courses. Current navigation has 109+ links, creating cognitive overload.
5. **Increase returning buyer rate** — Someone who completed one course should easily find and buy their next required certification.
6. **Modernize brand presence** — Position The Builders Academy as the UK's leading, most trusted online construction training provider. Current design is functional but dated.

### Tertiary Goals

7. **Increase organic search visibility** — Better SEO structure (heading hierarchy, structured data, internal linking) to capture more high-intent search traffic.
8. **Build an email/marketing pipeline** — Klaviyo is already integrated but underutilized. Opportunity for post-purchase sequences and employer nurturing.
9. **Reduce support burden** — Better course information, FAQ, and self-service reduces reliance on Tawk.to live chat for pre-purchase questions.

## 4. Risks to Success

### Critical Risks

1. **Magento platform constraints** — Redesign must work within Magento/Adobe Commerce. Custom themes, checkout flows, and navigation changes are possible but require Magento-specific development. Fantasy redesigns that ignore platform reality will fail.

2. **Low digital literacy audience** — The primary audience includes older tradespeople (40-60+) with limited tech confidence. Over-designing, using subtle UI patterns, or requiring complex interactions will lose this audience. Simplicity is not optional — it's a hard requirement.

3. **Mobile-first reality, desktop-first design** — Significant traffic comes from mobile devices. If the redesign prioritizes desktop aesthetics over mobile usability, conversion will drop. Mobile checkout friction is the biggest single revenue risk.

### High Risks

4. **Trust erosion during transition** — Existing customers have mental models of the current site. A dramatic visual change could temporarily reduce trust. The redesign should feel like an evolution, not a revolution.

5. **Navigation overload** — Current site has 109+ navigation links. Simplifying could improve discovery for new users but confuse returning customers who know where things are. Needs careful testing.

6. **SEO ranking disruption** — URL changes, heading structure changes, or content reorganization could temporarily impact search rankings. Migration strategy must preserve existing SEO equity.

### Moderate Risks

7. **Employer journey gap** — There is currently no visible employer/bulk purchase pathway. Adding this is a significant new feature, not just a redesign task. Requires product decisions about pricing, account management, and invoicing.

8. **Scope creep** — 10-phase plan is ambitious. Risk of losing focus on high-impact changes in favor of comprehensive but lower-priority improvements. Prioritization discipline is critical.

9. **Measurement gaps** — Several KPIs currently have no baseline. Without before/after data, it will be impossible to prove the redesign's impact. Analytics setup should happen before any changes go live.

## 5. Competitive Context

The UK online construction training market includes competitors like:
- **CITB** (the certification body itself)
- **ConstructionHelpline.com**
- **Safety Training Awards**
- **TrainingExpress.org.uk**

The Builders Academy differentiates through:
- Same-day certification
- CPD accreditation across the board
- Partnership with major employers (G4S, BBC Studios, Skanska, Kier, Wates)
- Competitive pricing (courses from £35)
- 24/7 access with unlimited free retakes

**Strategic opportunity:** None of these competitors excel at design or UX. A modernized, trust-forward, mobile-optimized experience would be a genuine competitive advantage in a market where most sites look outdated and transactional.

---

## 6. Strategic Constraints

- **Platform:** Magento/Adobe Commerce — all designs must be implementable
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.2 AA compliance is mandatory, not optional
- **Mobile-first:** Design for mobile first, enhance for desktop
- **Trust-forward:** Every design decision must increase perceived trustworthiness
- **Incremental:** Changes should be deployable incrementally, not as a big-bang launch
- **SEO-safe:** Preserve existing search ranking equity during transition
