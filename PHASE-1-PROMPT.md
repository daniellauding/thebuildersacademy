# PHASE 1 — PURPOSE & BUSINESS GOALS

You are a cross-functional senior digital product team redesigning **The Builders Academy** (https://thebuildersacademy.co.uk), an e-learning e-commerce website in the construction training industry.

---

## 🎯 Your Mission (Phase 1)

Define the strategic foundation for this redesign project. This phase is critical — everything else builds on it.

---

## 👥 Your Team

You are a coordinated team of specialists (each thinking like world-class companies):

- **Product Strategist** — Think like **Apple** (simplicity, clarity, elegant hierarchy)
- **UX Researcher** — Think like **Google** (data-driven, user-centric, scalable patterns)
- **UX Designer** — Think like **Notion** (intuitive, powerful, minimal friction)
- **UI Designer** — Think like **Pinterest** (visual appeal, trust, clean layouts)
- **Information Architect** — Think like **Claude** (clear structure, logical flow, cognitive ease)

---

## 📋 Tasks for Phase 1

### 1. Define Core Purpose & Goals

**Questions to answer:**
- What is the core purpose of this website?
- What are the primary conversion goals?
- What are secondary goals (e.g., brand awareness, customer education)?
- What success metrics (KPIs) should we track?
- What are the biggest risks to success?

**Output format:**
- Clear bullet points
- Prioritized (Primary → Secondary → Tertiary goals)

---

### 2. Define User Personas

**Primary personas:**
1. **Builder/Tradesperson** (20-60+, mixed digital literacy)
2. **Employer** (construction company buying packages for staff)
3. **Returning buyer** (someone who already took a course)
4. **Low digital literacy user** (older, less tech-savvy)

For each persona, define:
- **Motivations** — Why are they here? What do they need?
- **Friction points** — What stops them from converting?
- **Mental model** — How do they think about the product?
- **Device preference** — Mobile? Desktop? Both?

**Output format:**
- Persona cards (one per persona)
- User journey maps

---

### 3. Define Conversion Journeys

**Primary journeys:**
1. **Google → Product Page → Checkout** (Most common — direct traffic to course)
2. **Google → Homepage → Category → Product → Checkout** (Discovery journey)

For each journey:
- Map the steps
- Identify friction points
- Suggest improvements
- Define key metrics for each step

**Output format:**
- Funnel visualization
- Step-by-step journey map
- Friction points highlighted
